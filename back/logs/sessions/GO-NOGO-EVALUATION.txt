WHAT WOULD FIX THE NO-GO:
1. Toxic fills: tighten toxic detection threshold or add cooldown on first toxic fill.
   Current: cooldown only on toxic fill event. The issue is small-quantity fills that
   barely qualify as toxic. Could be reduced by:
   - Stricter fill model (don't fill within-tick SELLs when bid is far from order price)
   - Immediate cooldown on any toxic fill (already implemented, but cooldown is 15 cycles)

2. Concentration: add a hard cap that prevents any market from receiving fills once
   it reaches 35% fill share. Or increase market discovery/eligibility.

3. Consecutive negatives: reduce guardMaxConsecutiveNegative from 10 back to 6-8.
   Current threshold is too permissive for markets that lose consistently.

=====================================================
FILL MODEL CONCERN (major)
=====================================================
Fill rate of 99.8-99.9% is unrealistically high. In real Polymarket:
- Fill rates are typically 5-20% for passive limit orders.
- The shadow fill model uses:
  * bid_reached_quote (BBO moved to order price) -- credible
  * bid_touched_within_tick (BBO within 0.01 of order) -- somewhat generous
  * mid_through fills -- none observed (good)

The high fill rate means shadow PnL numbers are NOT directly predictive
of real-money performance. Real fills would be much fewer, and the PnL
would be proportionally lower. The directional signal (positive PnL) is
valid, but the magnitude is inflated.
